# Stitch Optimization Prompt

Optimize the attached initial concept only. Do not restart the design or change the product boundary.

Product:
PDF Page Remover — a single-function utility for deleting pages from a PDF.

Core flow:
Upload PDF → select pages to delete → delete selected pages → download result

Hard constraints:
- Keep it strictly focused on PDF page deletion only
- Do not add merge, split, compress, OCR, sign, convert, edit, rotate, or any suite/platform behavior
- Do not make it feel like a SaaS landing page
- Do not use decorative illustrations, 3D art, or emoji-style icons
- Trust, limits, compliance, and FAQ must remain secondary
- One dominant CTA only depending on state: Upload PDF or Delete Selected Pages

Visual direction:
- Light background, dark text
- Soft borders, subtle shadows, restrained radius
- One stable accent color for CTA and processing states
- Clean, practical, trustworthy, document-utility feel
- Avoid busy gradients, oversized hero sections, and generic SaaS chrome

Homepage structure:
1. Minimal top bar with brand and a few low-emphasis links
2. Hero with short headline, concise subheadline, and one clear CTA
3. Realistic utility preview card showing:
   - PDF file card
   - page thumbnails
   - selected-page chips/badges
   - delete-selected state
   - small ready/processing indicator
4. Three simple value-prop cards
5. Three-step “How it works” section
6. File limits / usage boundaries
7. Anonymous use
8. Lightweight compliance/trust strip
9. FAQ link or compact FAQ
10. Final CTA

Hero requirements:
- Left: title, subheadline, CTA, small trust line
- Right: realistic processing/preview card
- Show a visible summary like “3 pages selected for deletion”
- Support empty, uploading, scanning, preview-ready, processing, and error states

Copy direction:
- Headline: “Remove PDF pages in seconds”
- Subheadline: “A focused tool for deleting unwanted pages from a PDF. Upload a file, mark the pages to remove, and download the result.”
- Keep copy short, direct, and utility-driven

Value props:
- Delete only what you do not need
- Anonymous use available
- Free for small files; credits may be required for heavier jobs

How it works:
1. Upload PDF
2. Select pages to delete
3. Download result

Limits section:
- Free: up to 20 MB and up to 200 pages
- Max: up to 100 MB and up to 500 pages
- Password-protected or encrypted PDFs are not supported
- Credits may be required for heavier jobs
Frame this as a practical boundary, not a pricing pitch

Anonymous use:
- Keep this short and credible
- Say the tool can be used without signing in
- Do not overstate anonymity

Compliance / trust:
- Keep Privacy, Terms, Cookie links visible but lightweight
- Analytics off by default
- This area must not compete with the main workflow

Mobile:
- Keep the CTA visible above the fold
- Stack the hero cleanly
- Make the upload/preview card responsive
- Preserve clear hierarchy and avoid clutter

States to support visually:
- Empty
- Uploading
- Scanning
- Preview ready
- Page selection
- Delete-selected
- Processing
- Success / download ready
- Over-limit
- Unsupported / encrypted PDF
- Credit insufficient
- Cookie consent

Final requirement:
This should feel like a focused, trustworthy PDF page-removal utility with a clear linear workflow. Optimize the existing concept; do not reinterpret the product as a suite, platform, or marketing site.
